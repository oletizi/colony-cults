Conviction of Marquis de Rays

Hawera & Normanby Star · 3 January 1884 · Papers Past

A — strips in a ROW (operator ask)

Reading English

CONVICTION OF MARQUIS DE RAYS. PARIS, January 2. The trial of the Marquis de Rays on charges of fraud and deception in connection with the New Ireland expedition concluded to-day, when tbe prisoner was found guilty and sentenced to four years' imprisonment, and to pay a fine of 8000 francs. The associates of the Marquis were also found guilty and sentenced to shorter terms of imprisonment, but fined in the same amount.
